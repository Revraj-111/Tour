Bathinda

Sadiyan Di Goonj | The Echo of Centuries

Eh Sirf Ik Shehar Nahi. Eh Ik Kahaani Hai.

This is not just a city. It is a story waiting to be told. Welcome to Tabar-i-Hind, the "Gateway to India" — a city built on layers of legend, wrapped in the warmth of Punjab da dil.

Yahan, ancient echoes don't fade; they harmonize with the city's vibrant, evergreen pulse. From the ramparts of Qila Mubarak to the golden fields of malwa, your journey into the heart of Bathinda begins now.

Qila Mubarak

The Blessed Fort | Itihaas Da Gawah

It is not a ruin; it is a witness. These kachhi-pucca walls have seen empires rise and fall. They held the ambitions of Raja Dab and the anguish of Razia Sultan. Built over 1800 years ago, this majestic fort stands as the beating heart of Bathinda's story — a testament to resilience, royalty, and the passage of time.

Gurudhwara & Temples

Sacred Sanctuaries | A Tapestry of Faith

Shanti has a voice here. It resonates in the sacred kirtans of Takht Sri Damdama Sahib, the oldest of the five Takhts. It whispers through the corridors of Maiser Khana Mandir and Lakhi Jungle. Bathinda is a tirtha sthal — a place of pilgrimage woven from the threads of Sikh, Hindu, and Sufi traditions.
